PS-TTS: CONTINUOUS EMOTION CONTROL AND TEXT-ADAPTIVE TEXT-TO-SPEECH
VIA PROSODY STEERING

Lang Duan1, Minchuan Chen2, Yan Shi2, Shaojun Wang2, Jing Xiao2
1Harbin Institute of Technology, China     2Ping An Technology, China

Abstract

Current emotion-controllable text-to-speech systems often rely on global embeddings or discrete labels independent of textual context, resulting in unnatural prosody and rigid expression. To address this misalignment, we propose PS-TTS, a framework for continuous and text-adaptive emotion control via prosody steering, building on a flow-matching framework with a DiT backbone. A text-adaptive prosody predictor conditioned on emotion label, intensity and text generates prosody vectors, which are injected into each DiT block through cross-attention for context-aware emotional steering. To support fine-grained control, we introduce a text-adaptive intensity modeling scheme that fuses emotion embeddings and textual representations to produce context-aware emotion intensities, allowing the same emotion to manifest differently across sentences. Furthermore, an auxiliary emotion consistency loss enforces stepwise alignment of intermediate states with target emotions, improving controllability and naturalness. Experiments show that PS-TTS outperforms strong baselines in both naturalness and emotion expressiveness, while providing continuous, context-sensitive, and fine-grained emotion control.
